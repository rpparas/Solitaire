Java Solitaire

Help...

  • Solitaire [top]
    The primary implementation-based class, Solitaire, holds a Vector-type data structure to store the 52 cards.  The Vector was chosen since there is a considerable reduction of cards as they are distributed to the Piles and the Stock.

  • Pile [top]
    The Pile is instantiated seven times, and each instance is stored as an array element.  The Pile utilizes the LinkedList data structure to hold the shuffled cards which have been distributed to it.

  • Stock [top]
    The Stock utilizes the LinkedList data structure to hold the shuffled cards (those left after distributing to the Piles).  It also uses the Stack ADT to store the three cards drawn at a time from those shuffled.

  • Cabinet [top]
    The Cabinet class is instantiated four times in accordance to the four card suits.  It makes use of the Stack data structure as it accumulates one card at a time and only releases the topmost card in its stack.
